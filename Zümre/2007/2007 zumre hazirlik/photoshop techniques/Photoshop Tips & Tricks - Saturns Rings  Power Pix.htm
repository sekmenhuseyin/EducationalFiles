Create Saturns Rings In Photoshop
Part: 1 - 2

Step 1: Start by creating a 150 pixel by 150 pixel image with white as the background.

Step 2: Then you will need to click the new layer icon shown in the red circle to create a new layer on top of the background.
Step 3: Click the round selection tool.

In marquee options, pick fixed size for the style and enter 130 width and 50 for height. Then click in your document window to create the selection.
Step 4: Change the foreground color to black. Pick the paint bucket tool (fill) and then click inside your selection.

Step 5: While the black oval is still selected, click select/modify/contract. This will bring up a dialog box so that you can specify the pixels that you want to decrease the selection by. In the popup window type 8 and then OK. You will see a smaller selection inside the original one.

Step 6: With the new selection still active, make a new layer. Change the foreground color to white and fill with white. ctrl-D (PC) command-D (Mac) to deselect. You will now have layer 1 with the black oval on it and layer 2 with a smaller white oval on it.

Step 7: Click edit / transform / rotate. A box will appear around your white oval. Move your mouse to the top right corner and rotate till you are relatively close to the image on the right. Click the move tool in the tool bar. A box will pop up asking you if you want to apply or discard the changes. If you like where you have rotated your little white oval, then click apply. If you want to try again, click cancel.
Step 8: Command (Mac) CTRL (PC) click layer 2 to select the white oval. Click on layer 1 and then hit delete key (or clear).
If you turn off layer 2 and the background you will see that you have cut away the inside of your black oval making an off center ring. Deselect your newly formed ring.
